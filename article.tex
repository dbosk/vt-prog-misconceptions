\section{Further misconceptions from the literature}

The following misconceptions are documented in the literature but not yet given a
full variation-theory treatment in the catalogue; we record them here as
candidates for future inclusion.

\begin{itemize}
    \item That a variable can hold more than one value at a time
      \autocite{Doukakis2007}. This relates both to the type system (confusing a
      list with a non-container type, not seeing a list as a type in its own
      right) and to scope (expecting the same identifier to mean different things
      in different scopes).
    \item That, even once students know a \mintinline{python}{return} statement
      is needed, they have difficulty returning the \emph{right} value or
      variable from a function \autocite{KumarVeerasamy2016} --- a refinement of
      the return-boundary misconceptions in \cref{misconceptions}.
\end{itemize}
